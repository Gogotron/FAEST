\begin{frame}{How do we prove products?}
  How do we prove that $a\cdot b=c$ from $\left<a\right>^1,\left<b\right>^1,\left<c\right>^1$?
  \pause
  \begin{itemize}
    \item Compute $\left<a{\cdot}b-c\right>^2=\left<a\right>^1{\cdot}\left<b\right>^1-\left<c\right>^2$, \\
      should be commitment to 0.
    \pause
    \item Prover reveals masked coefficients of the polynomial.
    \pause
    \item Verifier checks evaluation at $\Delta$ and leading coefficient.
  \end{itemize}
\end{frame}
