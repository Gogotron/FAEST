\begin{frame}{AES}
  $\mathsf{AES}$ is a Substitution Permutation Network \\
  \begin{enumerate}
    \item $\texttt{KeyExpansion}$
    \item 10-14 rounds, in each round:
      \begin{enumerate}
        \item $\texttt{SubBytes}$, quadratic operation $x\mapsto x^{-1}$ (and $0\mapsto 0$)
        \item $\texttt{ShiftRows}$, $\texttt{MixColumns}$, $\texttt{AddRoundKey}$, linear operations
      \end{enumerate}
  \end{enumerate}
  \pause
  \ \\\ \\
  Linear gates are free, all we need to prove are the $\texttt{SubBytes}$ inversions in each round and in the key expansion.
  \\\ \\
  \pause
  To prove that $y=x^{-1}$ or $x=y=0$, \\
  we prove that $x(xy-1)=0$ and $y(xy-1)=0$.
  \pause

\end{frame}
